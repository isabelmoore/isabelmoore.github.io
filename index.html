Using Sensor-Health-Aware Resilient Fusion for Localization in Presence of GPS Spoofing Attacks

Co-authored research on securing autonomous navigation against GPS spoofing using Sensor-Health Aware Resilient Fusion (SHARF). Validated the algorithm by fusing GPS, IMU, and onboard sensor data on a drive-by-wire Lincoln MKZ using Python and ROS2, demonstrating resilience during emulated attacks.

Status: Completed, Sep 2023 - Jun 2024

Technical lead for lateral and longitudinal control systems in the GM/SAE AutoDrive Challenge.

Investigated pressure drop in the Great Horned Owl (GHO) gas turbine combustor via CFD modeling and experimental validation. Identified critical geometry discrepancies, validated CFD predictions against cold flow testing, and characterized jetting issues affecting light-off conditions.

Conducted as part of the DOE University Turbine Systems Research (UTSR) Fellowship at Southwest Research Institute.
